I don’t believe much in regret.

Every decision we make is the most honest choice we could offer at that moment — shaped by who we were, what we knew, and what we were capable of then. If given the same moment again, I believe I would still choose the same path. And if that is true, what is there to regret?

This belief didn’t come from certainty, but from struggle. For many years, I lived with anxiety — the kind that makes breathing feel difficult, as if air itself must be earned. It took nearly ten years to understand it, live with it, and eventually overcome it.

I don’t see that time as something lost. It taught me patience, attention, and respect for quiet moments. Today, I move forward without urgency, without resentment toward the past — only with a clearer sense of presence, and gratitude for being able to breathe freely again.
