2026

《蝶恋花·茉莉渡江》

金风秋雪戏杨柳，万缕千丝，守乌纱发白。 共韵以观茶道砚，墨语沉香许流芳。 烛灯摇曳暖寒楼，日影微旧，长似水无穷。 愿往皓月同西东，曲书不赋天下人。

Jasmine Crossing the River

Autumn winds and falling snow tease the willows, Threads upon threads, guarding hair turned white beneath official caps. In shared rhythm, we watch tea and ink together, Words of fragrance sink deep, promising lasting echoes. Candlelight sways, warming the cold chambers, Sun shadows age softly, flowing without end. May we face the bright moon, east or west alike, Writing no songs for the world but only for ourselves.

2025

《日暮行·白梅冠雪》

夜阑流萤，昙花端安侍雨 枫秋晚亭，门阙怜忘清曲 归潮邃凌，岸舟侧问浪语 溯墨古今，朝颜古墙题序

White Plum Crowned with Snow

Fireflies drift through the late night, Ephemeral blossoms rest quietly beneath the rain. Autumn maples surround the evening pavilion, Palace gates listen to forgotten melodies. Tides return, deep and distant, Boats at the shore ask the waves their language. Ink traces past and present, Morning blooms write their names upon ancient walls.

《水穷》

墨晕挽宣白，琴萧沉阳关。 震行八万里，水云顶漫穹。 苍龙惊天雷，蹄马走疾风。 多山皆具荣，无往终南国。 悬起撼四方，落笔绝千言。

Where the Water Runs Out

Ink gathers and thins on white paper, Zither and flute fade into distant passes. I have moved through eighty thousand miles of wind and thunder, Water and cloud pressing against the vast sky. Azure dragons wake within the storm, Horses race beneath cracking light. Every mountain holds its glory. Every path finds its stillness. A single stroke is held in the air, And a thousand words fall silent.

2024

《何以歌·海棠葬雨》

褴褛着，韵观砚行孤墨 磷灯祝，往皓月随阡陌 傲雪醉，映万家如星落 鸿雁至，扬海浪化烟波

Begonia Buried in Rain

Ragged verses linger, Ink travels alone across the inkstone. Phosphor lamps offer silent prayers, The bright moon follows winding paths. Proud snow falls, A thousand households scatter like stars. Wild geese arrive, Lifting ocean waves into mist and smoke.

2023

《尹天矢·梨花问水》

清风在侧，彩云飘过染红的光阴似火 絮雪落覆，江波流去渐淡的往事如墨 晨露微薄，沾湿衣袖盘坐间妄自菲薄 晚尘叠霍，惊扰烛灯安思间难逾倥偬

Pear Blossoms Asking the Water

A quiet wind stays beside me, Colored clouds drift by, staining time a burning red. Snow-like petals fall, River currents carry the past away, Ink fading from memory’s page. Morning dew, light as breath, Dampens my sleeves as I sit, Lost in thoughts of my own smallness. Evening dust settles thick, Disturbing the candle’s flame, In stillness, my mind struggles to cross the night.

《望愈》

晴阳悄入百家窗，孤烟飘缕揽弧光。 闲花仍顾来年树，少年已非往旧人。

Toward Healing

Sunlight slips quietly into a hundred windows, A lone wisp of smoke gathers the curve of light. Idle flowers still look toward next year’s trees, Yet the youth I was is no longer the same.

2022

《毕业》

涩走年华踏川 斑驳陆离朝霞 殷勤百待逐浪 恰似锦州缠云

Graduation

We walk with bitterness through the years, crossing rivers of time. Dawn appears, fractured and scattered. With careful hope, we chase the waves ahead— Like clouds entwined above distant hills.

《硝火》

万盏烛花浸满城，沉醉战火漫疆痕。 逾展逾台逐鹿来，布首忧怨天不仁。

Embers of War

A city soaked in a thousand candle-flames, Intoxicated by war, its scars spreading across the land. Stage after stage, the hunt for power unfolds— While common lives grieve beneath an unmerciful sky.
